# Admissibility white paper — Pharos evidence under FRE 901 & 902(13)–(14)

> **Status.** This white paper states the design basis for admissibility and the
> verification procedure. The roadmap's exit bar is an *outside-counsel review* of this
> document and the evidence bundle against the Federal Rules of Evidence; that review is a
> legal gate (like SOC 2 and the third-party pen test). The cryptographic claims below are
> implemented and tested (`test/evidence.test.ts`, `test/integration.seal.test.ts`).

## What Pharos produces

Each governed agent action yields a signed, hash-chained `ActionRecord` sealed in WORM
storage (S3 Object Lock). A **claims pack** assembles a scoped record set plus a verification
bundle (public keysets, trusted-time anchors, and the documented procedure) that a
third-party expert validates **offline**, without trusting Pharos.

## FRE 901 — authentication

Rule 901(a) requires evidence "sufficient to support a finding that the item is what the
proponent claims." Pharos supports that finding cryptographically:

- **Integrity.** Each record's `contentHash = sha256(canonical(content))` and each record
  links to its predecessor via `prevHash`. Any alteration, insertion, deletion, or
  reordering breaks the chain (demonstrated by the tamper tests).
- **Source.** Each record is signed (Ed25519) by a per-tenant KMS key; the signature
  verifies against the published public key. Only the KMS-held private key could have
  produced it.
- **Time.** Chain heads are anchored by an **independent** timestamp authority (RFC
  3161-style), so the existence of the record set at a time does not rest on Pharos.

## FRE 902(13) — certified records generated by an electronic process

902(13) allows self-authentication of "a record generated by an electronic process or
system that produces an accurate result, as shown by a certification of a qualified person."
Pharos provides the basis: a deterministic, documented sealing process; a reproducibility
harness proving verdicts replay bit-identically; and a custody attestation in every pack.

## FRE 902(14) — certified data copied from an electronic device/file/storage

902(14) allows self-authentication of data "copied from an electronic device, storage medium,
or file, if authenticated by a process of digital identification." The claims-pack bundle is
exactly that: the records and a digital-identification process (hash + signature + anchor
verification) by which a qualified person certifies the copy matches the original.

## Selective disclosure (redaction) preserves authentication

Redacting a field for a given audience does not undermine authentication. Each payload field
is committed at seal time (`commitment = sha256(salt || value)`); the disclosure root is
signed and **bound to the record's contentHash**. A redacted view reveals (salt, value) for
shown fields and only the commitment for redacted fields; a verifier confirms the shown
fields and the signature without learning the redacted values. The unredacted original
remains intact in WORM under litigation hold.

## What a certifying expert attests

See [expert-verification-runbook.md](./expert-verification-runbook.md). In short: run the
documented procedure on the bundle; if every record's hash, signature, and chain link
verify, and the anchors' TSA signatures verify and cover the head, the expert can certify
the record set is authentic and unaltered since sealing.
